Update on 2010-12-03: Chrome update from version 7 to 8...
Update on 2010-11-29: I added many more modified demos, re-evaluated current results and put them in fancy table results. Changes done were mainly: adding needed namespaces, adding units to font sizes, dropped embedded SVG fonts, replaced stylable entities by direct inline styles and replaced ASV specific scripting interfaces by DOM standard ones.
MSIE9 is also evaluated, although its known lack of support for SMIL and FILTERS, and majority of these demos relies on those technologies, makes him obvious looser.

All demos there are slightly (more or less) modified examples from Adobe SVG samples site.
Although they are pretty old, I found them still quite exciting and good to test/demonstrate level of implementation in recent web browsers.
They cannot be viewed directly from Adobe site, because of ASV plugin scripting requirements, often missing namespaces, runtime plugin architecture expectations and many more reasons. What I did is that I partially and very quickly repaired what was obvious, sometimes simplified something and put them in easy and runnable state there.

There is of course plenty of other improvements or workarounds which could be done, to have them working better, but it is task for another day ;-)
In percents under browser name I've expressed my personal opinion on how well each demo works.
